# X-ray Binary Time Dilation

## Relativistic Clocks in the Milky Way

This repository contains the manuscript and supplementary materials associated with the study:

**A Comparative Catalogue of Gravitational and Orbital Time-Dilation Effects in Galactic X-ray Binary Systems**

The project investigates how gravity, orbital motion, and black-hole spin influence the passage of time in compact-object binary systems across the Milky Way.

---

## Scientific Motivation

According to Einstein's General Theory of Relativity, clocks run at different rates depending on gravitational potential and velocity. X-ray binaries containing black holes and neutron stars provide natural laboratories for studying these relativistic effects under extreme astrophysical conditions.

This study presents a comparative catalogue of gravitational and orbital time-dilation effects derived from observational parameters reported in the astrophysical literature.

---

## Highlights

- Analysis of 18 Galactic X-ray binary systems
- Comparison of black-hole and neutron-star binaries
- Calculation of Schwarzschild radii
- Fixed-altitude gravitational time-dilation estimates
- Orbital relativistic clock-lag calculations
- Kerr black-hole spin corrections at the ISCO
- Monte Carlo uncertainty propagation
- Comparative ranking of relativistic time-dilation effects

---

## Main Results

The analysis demonstrates that relativistic clock-rate differences vary significantly across Galactic X-ray binaries.

Key findings include:

- Strong dependence of time-dilation effects on orbital compactness
- Significant enhancement of relativistic effects in rapidly spinning black-hole systems
- Donor-star clock lags ranging from minutes to hours per century depending on system parameters
- Extreme gravitational time-dilation effects near compact objects
